Where the metal in your phone comes from

A short, visual walk from rock in the ground to the magnets and batteries in your pocket — and why one step in the middle matters most.

Prepared 2026-06-24 · sources at end

1 — The short version

Your phone is a small pile of refined metal. The hard part isn't digging the rock up; it's the middle step that turns crude rock into pure, usable metal. A few numbers frame the whole story:

~30metals in one phone
99.9%purity refining must hit
~90%of magnet metals refined in one country

2 — How rock becomes phone metal

The journey is a pipeline. Smelting (using heat to melt ore and pull out a crude metal) and refining (purifying that crude metal to 99.9%) are the two middle steps that do the real work — and the steps that are hardest to build.

Why this matters: whoever owns the refining step owns the metal, even if the rock is dug up somewhere else.

5 — Where this simple story breaks

Two honest caveats. First, the exact percentages above move year to year and by phone model — treat them as rough. Second, "one country controls refining" is true today but is changing fast as other governments fund their own plants. The direction is clearer than the numbers.

The one thing to remember

The mine is not the prize. The refining step in the middle is — that's where the difficulty, the money, and the dependence all sit.
